Melbourne is plagued with un-useable routes. My personal threshold for a useable service is a 30 minute 'bare minimum' frequency which should be reserved for local routes. However, that's an extremely common arterial frequency, with many bus routes suffering from 40-60 minute or worse waiting times. 

The ['Bus Plan'](https://www.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2023-09/victorias-bus-plan-bus-reform-roadmap.pdf), released in 2021 with the linked roadmap published in 2023, aims to deliver "more efficient and fit-for-purpose bus network," planning to solve a lot of the issues that come from a generation of under-investment. 

However, the [2026 Inquiry](https://www.audit.vic.gov.au/report/improving-bus-services/?section=) into the Bus Plan by the Victorian Auditor-General's Office importantly found the following: 

> ...Incremental changes to local bus networks and FlexiRide services cannot deliver the scale of reform described in the Bus Plan.
>
> 63 per cent of people will walk up to, or over, 10 minutes (or about 800 metres) to a bus stop if the bus is frequent, fast and reliable.
