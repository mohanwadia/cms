Connections are understandably the most hated aspect about the viability of commuting on public transport. They make passengers walk and wait for an uncontrollable duration of time with varying infrastructure.

### Train to Train

With a commuter-focused radial network, Melburnians aren't too familiar with changing trains. This is evident through the vocal Dandenong Group regulars recently being removed from regular service at the popular South Yarra and Richmond stations, requiring a change of trains at either Caulfield, Malvern, or Town Hall / Flinders Street. 

The popular CBD stations are built for high-capacity pedestrian throughfare, visible real-time information on how to navigate, and wayfinding. The three City Loop stations only have four stacked platforms with very small distances require to change platforms and hence trains. Southern Cross and Flinders Street have multiple escalators (of varying reliability) to take you up to the concourse or below the platform to change trains.

However, the major multi-modal interchange Caulfield Station was not adequately prepared for passengers on two of the most frequent and productive lines. [Taitset's Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3duRRt8OLh8) on a new interchange for the station. 

### Bus Interchanges

A bus route that operates three buses an hour sounds way better when you omit regular 30-minute gaps (cough cough 903), however bus connections with routes duplicating bus corridoors creates this exact dilema for transferring passengers.
