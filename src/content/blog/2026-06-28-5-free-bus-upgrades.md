I've ensured that each of these changes can happen in isolation. All frequencies are weekday inter-peak.

## #1 - 900 Caulfield RS - Ferntree Gully RS

**Resources:** 900 (All), 691 (All)  
**Frequency:** 15min

Using the resources from the 691, the very popular Route 900 can be extended to Ferntree Gully Station at an existing 15 minute frequency along Kelletts Rd and Napoleon Rd, serving way more people in Rowville. If needed, resources from Routes 691/692/Flexiride could be used additionally.

Jacksons Rd (Mulgrave) and Waverley Gardens SC will lose a 30-minute frequency one-seat ride to Rowville, however it will be just as fast to catch the [upgraded 802/804](https://www.premier.vic.gov.au/bus-bonanza#:~:text=Better%20connections%20from%20Dandenong%20to%20Chadstone%20and%20Monash%20Uni%20(Routes%20802%2C%20804%20and%20862)) and connect to the 900. On the other hand, a large minority of Rowville will gain access to a one-seat ride to Monash Uni, Huntingdale RS, and Chadstone SC.

## #2 - 922/923 Southland - Elsternwick

**Resources:** 922 (All), 923 (All)  
**Frequency:** 20min

By combining and shortening the 922/923, a 20 minute frequency can be achieved using the same resources, creating a high-frequency route. Going Via Cheltenham RS, Sandringham RS, and Brighton Beach RS, unique coverage will connect residents to their closest train station/s.

### New 804: Huntingdale RS - Dandenong

**Resources:** 804 (All)  
**Frequency:** 20min

The new 804 was budgeted to form a 30 minute frequency between Chadstone SC and Dandenong RS. However, I propose shortening the route to Huntingdale RS in order to provide a 20 minute frequency. This will allow those travelling to Monash Uni / City with faster access, while those travelling to Oakleigh / Chadstone will require changing buses however will likely result in shorter travel times.

As the Wellington Rd corridor will already have two 15-min frequency routes to Chadstone, I propose sending the new 804 via Police Rd and Wanda St, providing a better service towards the City and Monash Uni. Residents wanting to travel to Springvale unable to walk to Springvale Rd should changes buses.

### New 814/885: Parkmore - Huntingdale RS

**Resources:** 885 (2 buses), 814 (2 buses)  
**Frequency:** 30min

Coverage between Parkmore and Noble Park is provided a much needed high-frequency route connecting Noble Park and Keysborough residents to their nearest train station. Noble Park North along Jacksons Rd is importantly also upgraded from an hourly service.

### New 850/848: Glen Waverley - Dandenong

**Resources:** 850 (4 buses), 848 (2 buses)  
**Frequency:** 20min

A big upgrade for Wheelers Hill and Noble Park North residents.

The 848 already has low patronage, so a reutilization of it's resources is much needed.

## #4 - 703 Split + Extension

**Resources:** 703 (All), 631 (6 buses)  
**Frequency:** 15min

Using the 631 resources, 703 West will go via Clayton RS, Monash Uni, M-City SC, and terminate at Waverley Gardens Via Police Rd.

703 East will terminate at Clayton RS in the south and be extended to the popular Box Hill RS/SC Via Whitehorse Rd in the north, still stopping at Blackburn RS.

## #5 - 281/293 Box Hill - Templestowe Village Via Doncaster SC

**Frequency:** 15min

Merging these routes provides a high-quality 15-minute frequency along Elgar Rd, as well as an upgraded north-south route between Templestowe and Doncaster SC (Via High St and Manningham Rd) which provides unique coverage.

## #6 - 693 Oakleigh - Ferntree Gully RS

**Resources:** 693 (All), 753 (All)  
**Frequency:** 15min  
**Losers:** Access to Glen Waverley, Watsons Rd residents.

These two routes duplicate each other along long stretches of Ferntree Gully Rd. Combining them provides a 15 minute frequency.

## #7 - 767 Box Hill - Chadstone

**Frequency:** 20min

Coming from Box Hill, the 767 will go via Huntingdale Rd and Atherson Rd to Oakleigh, then terminate at Chadstone. This will provide more coverage, an easier way to get to Monash University and train services.

## #8 - 626/627/822 into two high-quality north-south routes.

**Resources:** 626 (All), 627 (All), 822 (All)

At the moment, these three routes provide local service for areas that lack high-quality north-south routes to connect key destinations. Two straightened routes will provide a better service to the Frankston and Dandenong Lines as well as other key destinations.

### New 626: Gardiner RS - Southland Via Carnegie RS

**Frequency:** 20min

From Gardiner RS, it will run along Malvern Rd past Glen Iris RS and Darling RS, heading south along Darling Rd / Kooyong Rd / Tucker Rd past Carnegie RS to Moorabin RS, continuing along Nepean Hwy to Southland.

### New 627: Chadstone SC - Southland RS Via Murrumbeena RS

**Frequency:** 20min

From Chadstone SC, it will run along Princes Hwy, heading south along Murrumbeena Rd / East Boundary Rd / Rowans Rd Via Murrumbeena RS to Southland.
